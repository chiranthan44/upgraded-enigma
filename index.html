Reviews

  1. Name of the Wind by Patrick Rothfuss:

    This book is my absolute favorite. As someone who enjoys fantasy fiction, this novel stands out for its intricate world-building and compelling characters. The author, Patrick Rothfuss, creates a universe so rich that it feels almost real. The protagonist, Kvothe, is a multi-dimensional character, and his journey is one of self-discovery and survival. What truly struck me is the immersive experience Rothfuss offers; I felt as if I were living in the world alongside Kvothe, witnessing his triumphs and struggles. The storytelling is poetic, and I couldn’t put it down. If you love epic fantasy that blends magic, adventure, and a deep exploration of character, this book is for you.

  2. Music - Khalid (Free Spirit):

    Khalid’s Free Spirit album holds a special place in my heart. His soothing voice and soulful melodies are the perfect backdrop to both my quiet and reflective moments as well as times when I need a burst of energy. Songs like "Talk" and "Better" have such a smooth vibe that they seem to ease whatever mood I’m in. This playlist has been my go-to for various moods, from uplifting days to more somber, introspective ones. Khalid's ability to blend pop, R&B, and soulful sounds makes him a standout artist, and his work continues to resonate deeply with me.

  3. Shameless (TV Show):

    Shameless is a chaotic masterpiece, and it never fails to keep me hooked with its wild family dynamics. The show, centered around the Gallagher family, captures the gritty, emotional, and often hilarious struggles of living in a working-class neighborhood. What I love most is the unfiltered portrayal of family life, with its ups, downs, and everything in between. The characters feel so real, flawed, and human, making you both laugh and cry. The rawness of the show, paired with its humor, makes it unique in a way that I haven’t seen from many TV shows. While I wish I had more time to rewatch it, it remains one of my all-time favorites.
